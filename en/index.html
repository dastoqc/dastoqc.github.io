INTERFERENCE

It is from the disciplinary encounter between artistic and scenographic approaches that material and spatial blurring tints my creative work. By manipulating text as the primary material, the text material; the "intimate" materials and "cultural" materials and spatialization, my installations explore the interrelationships of my two passions.
From reading to the uprooting of phrases, I dissolve the text-material through my creative process, bringing out temporality and its fragility. It is by assembling objects that contain a story (intimate, utilitarian, and cultural) and materials (organic and sensitive) that I provoke situations of interference between resonance and sensory dissonance. The fragments of text intertwined with objects lead me to reveal the potential for strangeness and poetry.
I engage in this game that links the visible and the invisible, which stirs up the matter evacuated and retained from my artistic process. This disciplinary collision allows me to question the influence of words on the act of creation. Thus, I bring the question of intellectual property and the incompleteness of the literary text to the heart of my approach. Do the artist and scenographer have certain power over words? Can I manage to retain a meaning with the literary work affected/engaged, despite some detachment from the text? Does the distance between textual material and discipline have an impact on the spectator? Does the installation then take on a new form of literary work?
The scenographic codes that traverse the disciplinary framework create interference in the mechanisms of my installations. Whether on a temporal, material (physical), and spatial level, each acts and questions the choices I support and impose on the viewers. I thus address this contribution and the artistic impacts that intervene in the perception and reception of my installations.
